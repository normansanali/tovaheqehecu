Kenley Jansen Biography

Kenley Jansen is a baseball pitcher from Curaçao who currently plays for Los Angeles Dodgers in Major League Baseball. He played in the major leagues before being promoted to the Dodgers on July 2010. He has been honored with 2016 and 2017 All-Star and NL Reliever of the Year, 2017 NL saves leader. Similarly, he has also recorded the Pitched an immaculate inning on May 18, 2017.

On 17th November 2004, Jansen was signed by Los Angels Dodgers as an undrafted free agent. Following this, he played for North Shore Honu in the Hawaii Winter Baseball League, where he hit 121. In 9 games.

Again, he then played for West Oahu CaneFires in Hawaii Baseball winter. Afterward, he was selected to the mid-season Midwest League All-star game.

Jansen earned his promotion to the Dodgers on July 23, 2010, and made his major league debut against New York Mets on 24 July 2010. In his debut match, he pitched a scoreless inning, retiring all three batters.

Jansen then gained his first victory over Houston Astros on September 11, 2010. During his 2010 season, he played 25 games, working 27 innings with a 1-0 record and 0.67 ERA. He was named as the key member of the Dodgers bullpen in the 2011 season. During his 2014 season, Jansen became the fourth Dodger pitcher in history with 40th saves.
