Scott Peterson’s Early Life and Career

Before his infamous trial and conviction, Scott Peterson lived a relatively normal life. He was born in San Diego, California, and attended California Polytechnic State University, where he met his future wife, Laci. After graduating, he worked as a fertilizer salesman.

The Murder Case

In 2002, Laci Peterson, who was eight months pregnant at the time, disappeared. Scott Peterson was eventually arrested and charged with her murder and the murder of their unborn son. In 2004, he was convicted and sentenced to death.

Legal Costs and Financial Implications

The legal costs associated with Peterson’s trial were substantial. His defense team, led by high-profile attorney Mark Geragos, likely charged significant fees. Additionally, Peterson has been appealing his conviction for years, which also incurs considerable costs.

Book Deals and Media Rights

Despite his incarceration, Peterson has been the subject of numerous books and documentaries. While he may not directly profit from these, it is possible that he has sold rights to his story or received money from book deals.

Victim Compensation Fund

Current Financial Status
