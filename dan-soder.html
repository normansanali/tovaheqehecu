Dan Soder’s Parents, Siblings, and Extended Family

Dan Soder was born on June 24, 1983, to Patricia Susan Soder and Gary William Soder. He also had a sibling sister who died in a car crash when he was sixteen. Unfortunately, he had lost his father to alcoholism two years prior to the incident. As a result, Dan was largely raised by his family’s single mother.

Having said that, Dan credits both of his parents for his humorous genes, claiming that his father provided the raw sense of humor and his mother provided the delivery and theatrics.

In addition, the mother-son team agreed that if Dan performed in Denver, his mother would not tell him which show she was attending so that she could see her son perform without hesitation.

Dan Soder’s Net Worth, TV Shows, and Films

Dan Soder had no intention of pursuing a career in comedy, but fate had other plans for him. The idea for a comedy show came to the “Not Special” host when he was working on a dock in Alaska.

He happened to spend his summer with his aunt there, and during his time there, he made the people working with him laugh a lot.Dan made the decision to try comedy right then and there, and it was a game-changer for him. He began doing open mics and, in 2015, he co-hosted a Comedy Central radio show called “The Bonfire” with Jay Oakerson.

He became so famous that he was given his first-hour special, “Not Special.” From there, he went on to star in “The Standups” on Netflix and “Son of a Gary” on HBO. Aside from that, Dan appeared in a number of films and earned acting credits under his own name.
